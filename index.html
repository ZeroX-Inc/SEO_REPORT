First Year Academy

GROWING THE PIPELINE FROM DAY ONE

First Year Academy is an early intervention for first-year college students who have declared or plan to declare a major in Computer Science. In this exploratory training program, 75+ participants learn and apply Python coding fundamentals to build their own project with Codio and Pygame. Participants who complete the First Year Academy will have mastered the core computer programming fundamentals required to successfully apply and gain admission into SEO Tech Developer following completion of their sophomore year.

INSTRUCTION & COACHING

MENTORSHIP

Throughout the summer, students are paired in small groups with an experienced Engineering Manager (EM) who works closely with them on technical and professional development skills. This one-on-one support ensures individualized guidance for each student's career goals. The EMs come from software engineering and project manager backgrounds, and many have CS teaching experience that enables them to proactively meet their students' needs.

PROJECTS

The Summer Residency culminates in a final project where students showcase the skills they learn in frontend and backend development. Students are expected to adhere to industry-standard project management frameworks and SWE standards. They work in groups of 3-5, meeting regularly with their EMs to guide their progress. Once students deploy their projects online, external judges review their work and provide tech leadership roles review their work and provide feedback and mentorship. Projects function as a realistic gateway for students from academic backgrounds to apply lessons from the curriculum.
